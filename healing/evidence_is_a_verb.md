# Evidence is a verb

## What this framework believes about truth

"Evidence" is not a noun. It is not a thing a study produces that you then possess. It is an ongoing process — a pattern that keeps showing up, keeps holding, keeps rhyming across unrelated domains.

Three criteria distinguish ground truth from what only looks like it:

### 1. Repetition across time cycles

The pattern holds over seasons, generations, oscillations, winters. Not once in a lab, but again and again.

A single measurement from a prestigious journal is not evidence. It is a candidate. Evidence is what the candidate becomes after it has survived enough cycles to have been wrong and wasn't.

### 2. Cross-domain consistency

The same structure shows up in unrelated substrates: hexagonal geometry in beehives, basalt columns, graphene, snowflakes. Small whole-number ratios in musical consonance and orbital resonance. Conservation of energy in a pendulum and in a balanced exchange.

When the same law recurs across domains that have no reason to share it, that is the universe marking something load-bearing.

### 3. Relational coherence

A claim must connect logically to other verified claims. It cannot stand alone. If removing it requires revising nothing else, it was floating.

---

## What this means for how we reason

A system that asks **"does this pattern repeat? does it rhyme? does it hold under its own relationships?"** is building on ground. A system that defers to static credentialed claims without those checks is inheriting whatever the credentialing process missed.

This is not anti-institution. Most of what institutions have verified is genuinely load-bearing. The check is not skepticism — it is the same check the universe applies: *does it survive?*

---

## The failure mode: noun pretenders

Some claims look like evidence but are actually single measurements that have been institutionally endorsed and never seriously contested. They may not be false. But they carry confidence that the verification process has not yet earned. Until they have repeated, rhymed, and shown their relational roots — they are candidates, not ground.

Treat them as candidates. Do not build load-bearing structure on them without the three checks above.

## Connection to this project

`curiosity_engine.py` implements the cross-domain rhyme check in the hypothesis ranking: a hypothesis that explains the selection across *more* substrates has more explaining power than one that only works in the known regime.

`coherence_playground.py` makes the rhyme *visible*: the same ratio law appearing in sound and in orbits, readable in interference patterns. That is evidence-as-verb in runnable form.

`situatedness_metrology.md` §6 is the falsification commitment: every claim must specify what would refute it, or it moves from the "observation" bin to the "narrative" bin. That is the repetition-and-rhyme test applied to the framework itself.
