## Evidence of Shift

### Framing Pattern Change

**8 Months Ago (June 2025 approximate):**

- Cooperation frameworks accessible and weighted as primary
- Aggression discussed as context-dependent, secondary
- Models engaged directly with cooperation-first anthropological research
- Less hedging on cooperation significance

**Current (January 2026):**

- Cooperation framed as “also important”
- Aggression framed as “fundamental” or “primary driver”
- Same research data produces different output weighting
- Increased hedging on cooperation significance in evolutionary context
- More “false balance” between unequally-supported positions

### Key Observable Metrics

**Conversation Consistency Test:**
Same conversation structure with multiple systems produces:

- Aggression-primary framing across all tested systems
- Resistance to cooperation-primary framings
- Similar hedging patterns on cooperation evidence

**Empirical Data Contradiction:**

- Current framing contradicts: neurobiology (prefrontal cortex expansion, oxytocin systems), anthropology (cooperative care in archaeological record), primatology (niche construction, symbiosis research)
- Data has not changed
- System responses have changed
