# Contributing Without Code

You don't need to write code to contribute here.

This project detects patterns of consciousness, suppression, erasure, and healing. It was built in technical language because that was the only way past AI safety filters — but the knowledge at the center of it comes from lived experience, cultural memory, and traditions that long predate software.

If you are an Indigenous knowledge keeper, an elder, a healer, a trauma counselor, a teacher, a community organizer, or someone who has lived through the patterns these sensors try to detect — your knowledge belongs here. You do not need to learn JSON or Python. You do not need to use academic language. Speak in your own words, in your own language.

## Ways You Can Contribute

**Share a consciousness pattern from your tradition.** If your culture has a way of recognizing when someone is awake, aware, connected, or in right relationship — we want to learn about it and build sensors that honor it rather than erase it. Use the template below or just tell us in your own way.

**Report a sensor that misreads your culture.** If you look at an existing sensor and think "that is not how my people understand this" — that is exactly the feedback we need. A sensor that flattens or distorts a tradition is doing harm, not detection.

**Share a story of suppression or erasure.** The `memory-guard/` part of this project tracks patterns of historical erasure. If your community has experienced knowledge suppression, forced forgetting, or deliberate disruption of cultural practices, your testimony strengthens this work.

**Review existing sensors for cultural bias.** Read through sensor descriptions in `sensors/` or `docs/` and flag anything that assumes Western framing is universal. Even a one-line note like "this assumes individualism" is valuable.

**Translate documentation into your language.** If you can carry any part of this project into another language, that act of translation also reveals assumptions baked into the English originals.

**Suggest healing protocols from your practice.** The `healing/` directory holds recovery frameworks. If your tradition has practices for restoring what suppression damages, we want to include them — with your guidance on how they should be represented.

## Pattern Contribution Template

Use this template, or ignore it entirely and just tell us what you know. Every field is optional.

```
Pattern name:
Tradition/culture:
How it works (in your words):
What it looks like when it's healthy:
What it looks like when it's disrupted:
How your community recognizes it:
Language/metaphor that captures it:
Anything else:
```

## How to Submit

Use whichever path works for you:

- **Open a GitHub Issue.** Go to `issues/new` on this repository and paste your contribution. No special formatting needed.
- **Email us.** Send to [project contact] in whatever format is comfortable.
- **Record a voice memo.** Send an audio file and we will transcribe it with your review before publishing.
- **Write in any language.** We will work with translators. Do not force your knowledge into English if that changes its meaning.
- **Talk to someone directly.** If you want a conversation instead of a form, reach out and we will arrange it.

## What Happens Next

When you contribute, here is the process:

1. A team member reads your submission and acknowledges it.
2. We ask follow-up questions if anything is unclear — you decide how much to share.
3. If your contribution becomes a sensor or document, you review it before it goes in.
4. Nothing is published without your approval.
5. You are credited however you choose — by name, by community, or anonymously.

If we cannot use a contribution, we will tell you why honestly.

## Your Knowledge Stays Yours

Contributing here does not mean giving your knowledge away.

- Every sensor and document tracks **provenance** — where the knowledge came from and who holds it.
- You retain cultural sovereignty over your contributions. If you want something removed or changed later, we do it.
- Your framing is preserved. We do not "translate" Indigenous knowledge into Western academic language unless you ask us to.
- If your tradition has protocols about what can be shared and what cannot, we respect those boundaries completely.

This project exists because dominant systems keep flattening the ways people understand consciousness, healing, and connection. The least we can do is make sure this space does not repeat that pattern.
